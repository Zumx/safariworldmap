This is a practical list. Reserves are picked for the specific need — budget camping safaris — and ranked on the trade-offs that matter: experience, comfort, predictability of wildlife, and total cost. The order is roughly best-fit first, though every itinerary has its own constraints.

## 1. Kruger SANParks camps

Kruger SANParks camps is one of the strongest options for budget camping safaris. The reserve is well set up for this style of safari, with operators and staff who deliver the experience reliably. Practical considerations: book in advance for peak season, ask the operator directly about the specific activity (not just whether it 'might' be possible), and confirm vehicle and guide assignments before you fly.

## How to choose between them

If you can only pick one, weigh the most important constraint — budget, time window, family composition, photographic priority — against the strengths and weaknesses listed above. If you can do two, the strongest pairing combines a high-density reserve with a wilder, more remote one for contrast. A good ground operator will refine the choice; an indifferent one will sell you whatever is available on commission.
