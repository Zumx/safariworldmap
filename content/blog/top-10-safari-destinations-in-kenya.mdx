Kenya is the birthplace of the safari and still one of its greatest stages —
the Great Migration, classic savanna, and extraordinary diversity. These ten
are the essentials.

## 1. Maasai Mara National Reserve

Kenya's crown jewel — big cats year-round and the river crossings of the Great
Migration from roughly July to October.

## 2. Amboseli National Park

Huge elephant herds against the backdrop of Kilimanjaro — the iconic Kenyan
image.

## 3. Tsavo East & West

One of the largest wilderness areas in the world — red elephants and raw
scale.

## 4. Samburu National Reserve

Arid-north specialists found almost nowhere else — Grevy's zebra, gerenuk,
reticulated giraffe.

## 5. Lake Nakuru National Park

Flamingo-rimmed soda lake and a rhino sanctuary.

## 6. Laikipia / Ol Pejeta Conservancy

Community and private conservancies leading rhino conservation, with low-density
exclusive game viewing.

## 7. Lewa Wildlife Conservancy

A UNESCO conservancy and conservation flagship in the Laikipia highlands.

## 8. Meru National Park

Lush, remote, and historic — the "Born Free" country.

## 9. Aberdare National Park

Highland forest and moorland with unique tree-lodge game viewing.

## 10. Hell's Gate National Park

Rare walk- and cycle-safari terrain among gorges and geothermal cliffs.

## How Kenyan safari works

Mostly guided: lodge or tented-camp stays with game drives in a 4x4, often as a
multi-park circuit (Mara–Nakuru–Amboseli is classic). Conservancies around the
Mara and in Laikipia offer lower-density viewing, night drives, and walking
that the national reserves do not. Book through reputable operators.

## When to go

The dry seasons (late June–October and January–February) give the best
viewing; the Mara migration river crossings peak roughly July–October. The
"long rains" (March–May) are green, quiet, and cheaper but tougher for game
viewing.

## Safety and ethics

Stay in the vehicle, keep distance and quiet at sightings (especially the
migration crossings — do not block animals), choose operators that limit
vehicles per sighting, and favour conservancies and lodges that benefit Maasai
and local communities.
